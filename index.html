Theme of the Game

Your cards represent soldiers and leaders in the midst of a civil conflict. Old alliances have broken down, and even families fight amongst themselves, but you have risen above the fray to control one side in the epic battle. Strategy and luck will serve you well.

Setup

The standard 2-player Doomcrop game uses one deck of playing cards with the Jokers removed.

Shuffle the deck and deal 10 cards to each player, facedown.

Look at your cards and choose one to discard. Arrange your remaining cards into a 3-by-3 grid. You may look at your own facedown cards at any time.

Place the rest of the shuffled deck facedown between the two grids. This is the shared Draw Pile.

Take the card you discarded and place it face-up, separate from your opponent's face-up card. This is your individual Capture Pile.

Your Objective

Capture as many of your opponent's cards as you can. Whoever has the most cards in their Capture Pile by the time the Draw Pile runs out, wins.

Definitions

Capture - When you Capture a card, place it in your Capture Pile, face-up. The player whose card was Captured should immediately replace it with a facedown card from the top of the Draw Pile.

Captured cards do not leave the Capture Pile until they are counted out at the end of the game.

Opposing Card - Each card on your side of the board is opposed to one card under your opponent's control. Cards oppose each other if they have equivalent row height (front, middle, back), and the same vertical alignment (right, middle, left).

Flow of the Game

On your turn, you may take one of the following actions:

  1. Swap the places of any two of your own facedown cards.
  2. Turn one of your face-up number cards facedown (face cards cannot be turned facedown).
  3. Flip one of your cards face-up and activate its ability (face-up cards cannot be activated).

After you have resolved your action and replaced any Captured cards, pass the turn to your opponent.

Numbers Attack!

Statistically speaking, the deck will contain mostly Number cards. When you flip a Number face-up, it attacks its Opposing Card! If the Opposing Card is facedown, flip it face-up (this flip does not activate abilities).

  • If the Opposing Card is lower than your attacking Number (or if it's a tie), you capture the Opposing Card.
  • If the Opposing Card is higher than your attacking Number, your opponent captures your Number.

  • Face cards can only be captured by a Number of 8, 9, or 10.
  • If a Number between 2 to 7 attacks a Face, capture neither card. Both cards remain face-up.

Suit Abilities

Number cards also have special abilities depending upon their suit.

Hearts

When a Heart number card attacks, it can add the value of one of your own adjacent face-up Heart number cards to its own value until the attack is finished.

Diamonds

When you count out capture piles at the end of the game, Diamonds count for double (this includes Diamond face cards as well).

Clubs

When a Club number card attacks, you may choose to attack any card in its own column instead of its opposing card.

(Example. The 9 of Clubs in your left middle space can hit any one of your opponent's right top, middle, or lower card.)

Spades

You may capture one of your own Spade cards on your turn as long as it's face-up. This does not count as your action for the turn.

Face Card Abilities

Each type of face card has a unique ability that is activated when flipped face-up.

  • When you flip over a Jack, swap the positions of any of your cards, as many times as you like until satisfied. You may not turn any cards face-up or face-down during this process.
  • When you flip over a Queen, turn any amount of your other cards facedown.
  • When you flip over a King, flip all of your number cards face-up, then each of your number cards attacks its opposing card.
  • When you flip over an Ace, instantly capture any card on the board (does not need to be face-up).

Ending the Game

When the draw pile runs out, continue taking turns until both players agree there are no more actions worth taking. Leave the remaining cards on the board and count out your capture pile. The winner is the player with more cards in their capture pile, including doubled Diamonds.

In the event that capture piles are tied, or the players want to continue the game, shuffle your capture pile and your remaining cards together - this is now your individual draw pile. Deal yourself a new 3-by-3 square and continue playing until one capture pile runs out.


Optional Rules

Playing with Jokers

Unlike the other face cards, the Joker can be captured by any number card.
When you flip a Joker face-up, or when you capture a Joker, roll a 6-sided die and obey the table below.

  1. Players swap sides of the board.
  2. Players swap capture piles.
  3. Each player captures all of their own cards and deals a new 3-by-3 square.
  4. All face cards are activated, including face-up ones.
  5. Each player shuffles their own cards together and deals their 3-by-3 square randomly.
  6. For the rest of the game, play with the top card of the draw pile face-up
