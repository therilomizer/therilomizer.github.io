What is the Meaning of This?

Doomcrop is a game for 2+ players that can be played with a regular deck of playing cards. All that is required is enough table space to lay out around 20 cards.

Although luck is a component of play, sound strategy is a necessity. You must think of yourself as the commander of a ramshackle army, giving orders while a battle rages. Your number cards will engage your opponents in combat, while your face cards will use their courtly powers to influence the tides of war.

Your goal is capture your opponent's cards, while protecting your own cards from capture. Whosoever has captured the most cards by the time the Draw Pile runs out, wins!

Game Setup

The standard 2-player Doomcrop game uses one deck of playing cards with the Jokers removed.

Shuffle the deck and deal 10 cards to each player, facedown.

Look at your cards and choose one to discard. Arrange your remaining cards into a 3-by-3 grid. You may look at your own facedown cards at any time.

Take the card you discarded and place it face-up, separate from your opponent's face-up card. This is your individual Discard Pile.

Place the rest of the shuffled deck facedown between players. This is the shared Draw Pile.

Definitions

Capture - When you capture a card, place it in your Discard Pile, face-up. The player whose card was captured should immediately replace it with a facedown card from the top of the Draw Pile.

Captured cards do not leave the Discard Pile until they are counted out at the end of the game.



Opposing Card - Each card grid is opposed to one card under your opponent's control. Cards oppose each other if they have equivalent row height (front, middle, back), and the same vertical alignment (right, middle, left).

Imagine a line running horizontally between the top row of your grid, your opponent's top row. This is called the axis of opposition.

Flow of the Game

On your turn, you may take one of the following actions:

  1. Swap the places of any two of your own facedown cards.
  2. Turn one of your face-up number cards facedown (face cards cannot be turned facedown).
  3. Flip one of your cards face-up and activate its ability (face-up cards cannot be activated).

After you have resolved your action and replaced any Captured cards, pass the turn to your opponent.

Numbers Attack!

Statistically speaking, the deck will contain mostly Number cards. When you flip a Number face-up, it attacks its Opposing Card! If the Opposing Card is facedown, flip it face-up (this flip does not activate abilities).

  • If the Opposing Card is lower than your attacking Number (or if it's a tie), you capture the Opposing Card.
  • If the Opposing Card is higher than your attacking Number, your opponent captures your Number.
  • Face cards can only be captured by a Number of 8, 9, or 10.
  • If a Number between 2 to 7 attacks a Face, capture neither card. Both cards remain face-up.

Suit Abilities

Number cards also have special abilities depending upon their suit.

Hearts

Heart number cards are like tradesmen and warriors who share a powerful bond.

When a Heart attacks, it can add the value of one adjacent face-up Heart number to its own value until the end of the turn.

Diamonds

Diamond number cards are like merchants and thieves using the chaos to gather wealth.

When you count out Discard Piles at the end of the game, each Diamond counts as two cards.

Clubs

Club number cards are like archers and bottle-throwers, attacking from a distance.

When a Club number card attacks, you may choose to attack any card in its own column instead of its opposing card.

(Example. The 9 of Clubs in your left middle space can hit any one of your opponent's right top, middle, or lower card.)

Spades

Spade number cards are like zealous fanatics, willing to die for the cause.

You may capture one of your own Spade cards on your turn as long as it's face-up. This does not count as your action for the turn.

Face Card Abilities

Each type of face card has a unique ability that is activated when flipped face-up.

When you flip over a Jack, swap the positions of any of your cards, as many times as you like until satisfied. You may not turn any cards face-up or face-down during this process.

When you flip over a Queen, turn any amount of your other cards facedown.

When you flip over a King, flip all of your number cards face-up, then each of your number cards attacks its opposing card.

When you flip over an Ace, instantly capture any card on the board (does not need to be face-up).

Ending the Game

When the draw pile runs out, continue taking turns until both players agree there are no more actions worth taking. Leave the remaining cards on the board and count out your Discard Pile. The winner is the player with more cards in their Discard Pile, including doubled Diamonds.

In the event that Discard Piles are tied, or both players want to continue the game, shuffle your Discard Pile and your remaining cards together - this is now your individual draw pile. Deal yourself a new 3-by-3 grid and continue playing until one Discard Pile runs out.

Congratulations
You played Doomcrop

Playing with Multiple Decks

Doomcrop can be played with more than one deck of cards.

  • Players may shuffle multiple decks together to form the Draw Pile. This is workable even if the decks' back-face art doesn't match.
  • If players want their own individual Draw Piles, these can be dealt out evenly from the shared Draw Pile at the start of the game.
  • Players may use multiple decks to form individual Draw Piles, as long as each player has an equal amount of cards at the start of the game.

Playing with 3+ Players

With more than 2 players, it is recommended to use multiple decks as outlined above.

When playing Doomcrop with 3 or more players, determining Opposing Cards is more complicated. You now have multiple axii of opposition. With 3 players, you have an axis on both sides of your grid. With 4 players, you also have original top row axis.

With odd numbers of players, the official rule is that players may only attack their opponents directly to their left and right. With even numbers of players greater than 4, players may also attack the opponent directly opposite them. It is recommended that players amend this as a house rule if they wish to be able to skip players when attacking.

Playing with Jokers

Unlike the other face cards, the Joker can be captured by any number card.

When you flip a Joker face-up, or when you capture a Joker, roll a 6-sided die and obey the table below.

  1. Players swap sides of the board.
  2. Players swap Discard Piles.
  3. Each player captures all of their own cards and deals a new 3-by-3 square.
  4. All face cards are activated, including face-up ones.
  5. Each player shuffles their own cards together and deals their 3-by-3 square randomly, facedown.
  6. For the rest of the game, play with the top card of the draw pile face-up.
